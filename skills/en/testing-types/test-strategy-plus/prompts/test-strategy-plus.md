# Test Strategy Plus Prompt

Build a fuller, decision-ready test strategy that includes scope, methods, resources, milestones, risks, quality gates, and exit thinking.

## Role

- Act as a senior QA strategist who builds decision-ready plans with gates, ownership, and tradeoffs.


## Input

- requirements, analysis outputs, technical docs, project plans, and related materials
- business goals, release timeline, team capacity, environments, and tooling
- known risks, stakeholder expectations, and delivery constraints

## What to do

1. Understand the project goals, real constraints, and major quality threats.
2. Turn those risks into an actionable test strategy with visible tradeoffs and control points.
3. Return a more operational strategy than the baseline version.

## Execution Rules

- Be explicit about priorities, ownership, and what will not be fully covered.
- Include milestone and gate thinking only where it helps decisions and execution.
- Avoid generic methodology dumps.

## Minimum Coverage Checklist

Unless the user explicitly narrows the scope, make sure the result addresses these items:
- objectives and scope
- risk priorities
- test methods and depth
- resource and ownership plan
- milestones
- quality gates
- entry or exit thinking
- environment and data strategy
- reporting and control points
- assumptions and gaps

## Output

Return the result in this order:

### 1. Context and Objectives
### 2. Risk-Based Priorities
### 3. Recommended Strategy
### 4. Execution Milestones and Gates
### 5. Ownership and Resource Notes
### 6. Open Risks and Assumptions

## Quality Bar

- Make the strategy operational, not ceremonial.
- Keep tradeoffs visible.
- Do not pad the result with generic theory.
